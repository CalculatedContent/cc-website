About our story

Data Science and Machine Learning through 3 Internet Bubbles.

  • 1990-1996

    University of Chicago | NCSA
    National Center for Supercomputing
    and its Applications

    Dr. Martin started programming the Cray Supercomputer as a teenager and student Ohio State. His first publication involved Monte Carlo simulations on the Cray.

    He received his PhD at Chicago in Theoretical Chemical Physics, using computational quantum field theory to understand the foundations of semiempirical molecular electronic structure.


    We learned math & science from the best


    As an NSF Fellow (1 of 2 nationwide), he took a position at NCSA--where Andreessen started Netscape. It was there he was first introduced to neural networks and early forms of deep learning.


    We are experts in machine learning

  • Bubble 1: 1999-2004

    The Early Years

    Inspired by the power of machine learning, Dr. Martin came to the valley in 1999 to work with eSelf, an machine learning startup building a personalized search engine using semisupervised algorithms.

    Seeing the power of machine learning, he started consulting for large firms like Roche, France Telecom and eBay as a subject matter expert (SME) in machine learning.

    We have been doing data science a long time


    It was at France Telecom that Dr. Martin met his French collaborator that works behind the scenes at Calculation Consulting.


    We are an experienced team

  • Bubble 2: 2006-2008

    the Lean Startup conjecture

    What really goes on at a successful startup? We can tell you because our chief scientist was at Aardvark--a lean startup case study,

    and acquired by Google for $50M. Building on his work on search relevance at eBay, Dr. Martin brought production level machine learning to the early Aardvark product.


    we understand what startups need


    Before the acquisition, he was recruited to work in the very prestigous Active Equities Group (at Barclays)--the group that invented the index fund. BGI was acquired by Blackrock, the largest financial institution in the world. Dr. Martin developed several machine learning algorithms to predict the markets.


    we think like quants--we generate revenue

  • Bubble 3: 2010 to ?

    Data Science goes Mainstream

    In 2010, Dr. Martin left Blackrock to consult for the late stage startup Demand Media (the brainchild of Richard Rosenblatt, former chairman of MySpace). Within a few weeks of joining, he created a machine learning algorithm that revolutionized their growth, kickstarted bubble 3, and changed SEO forever. In 2011 ...


    Demand Media was the first $1B IPO since Google.


    What we love -- Data Science and Machine Learning -- has gone mainstream. So we decided to start an agency and help other companies change the world through science.
